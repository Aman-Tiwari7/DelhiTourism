Continental

The one – Le Meridian

Perfect place for your Sunday brunch with family and friends. With the great quality of buffet food, this luncheonette provides luscious continental dishes with super polite staff and lavish ambience makes this place a must visit.

Le Meridian, Windsor Place, Janpath, New Delhi ₹4,500 for two approx. See Directions

Ricos

One of the paradise for food lovers from Delhi north campus food joints. Great place for hangout. Ricos is a place where you can have a wide variety of continental, American, Mexican, Italian, Lebanese and good ambience.

2526, 1st Floor, Hudson lane ₹900 for two approx. See Directions

Ardor 2.1

This place will keep you coming back for more. The lip smacking menu list includes continental, north India, Chinese, and a variety of desserts to keep you wanting for more. Located on the outer circle of Connaught place area this place is perfect for office parties and get together.

N-55/56& 88/89, outer circle, Connaught place, New Delhi ₹2,000 for two approx. See Directions

Chinese

The China Kitchen

If you’re up for pampering yourself with the best Chinese delicacies, there’s no place better than The China Kitchen. This fine-dining restaurant is known for its traditional Chinese fare with a pinch of style. Their Peking Duck, Beggar’s Chicken & Dan-Dan noodles are a few of the best picks here.

Hyatt Regency Delhi, Bhikaji Cama Place, Ring Road ₹2,000 for two approx. See Directions

Royal China Delhi

Royal China Delhi's elegant interiors will surely transport you to the lands of Singapore & Hong Kong. They have combined traditional and contemporary elements of the Cantonese cuisine & all the ingredients used are freshly sourced and authentic. Their food is a delicious combination of taste and essence, and don’t forget to sample the Crispy Aromatic Duck & Dimsums when you land up here!

03, Lower Ground Floor, The Chankya Mall ₹2,500 for two approx. See Directions

House Of Ming - The Taj Mahal Hotel

This fine dining restaurant is one of our faves because of the super yum dishes which form a perfect balance of the hot, sweet and sour Chinese flavours. They make sure that each of their dishes is given a modern twist, keeping in mind their ethnicity. They use two of the finest Chinese cooking styles, Cantonese and Schezuan.When here, you should definitely try their scrumptious Jumbo Prawns, Diced Chicken with Cashew Nuts & for dessert, their Toffee Banana!

House Of Ming - The Taj Mahal Hotel - Mansingh Road ₹5,500 for two approx. See Directions

North Indian

Anardana

First up on our list is this swanky restaurant that has truly redefined luxury dining with stylish interiors, Portuguese tiles, and a stupendous bar. But that’s not their best bit. Guys, they serve the best North Indian food and literally nothing beats the zest of their North Indian delights and the quirky presentation. So, if you haven’t tried it yet, you really are missing out!

RK Puram & Karkardooma ₹1,800 for two approx. See Directions

Punjab Grill

Punjab Grill is hands down our go-to eatery for nummy North Indian cuisine and we truly vouch for their authentic flavours. These guys have a mouth-watering array of delight and some of their all-time favourites you should totally try are Raan-E-Sikandari, Tawa Tiger Prawn, Butter Chicken Kulcha, Kesar Malai Kofta and more.

Khan Market, Janpath, Aerocity, Pacific Mall ₹2,500 for two approx. See Directions

Garam Dharam

Owned by the legendary and evergreen superstar, Dharmendra, Garam Dharam is just the place to head over to when craving delicious North Indian spread. Also, this restaurant has retro-themed quirky interiors that will make the perfect backdrop for all your gorgeous pictures. From their menu, we defo recommend munching on the nosh-worthy Garam Dharam Special Soya Chaap, Makai Palak and the Balti Meat. Happy feasting!

Sector 18 Noida, Mohan Nagar Ghaziabad, & Murthal ₹1,800 for two approx. See Directions

South Indian

Juggernaut

This unique restaurant has idealized the whole notion of preserving our mother nature with a taintless environment. The interiors are inspired by the same, bringing classical values of families and social heritage together through art. Juggernaut offers appetizing South Indian delicacies to satiate your cravings for some authentic food.

Hs-16, Second Floor, Kailash Colony, New Delhi ₹550 for two approx. See Directions

Carnatic Cafe

Being one of the highest rated joints in the locality, this one serves scrumptious South Indian meals to make your day. A happy ambiance with a dedicated staff that will make you visit Carnatic Cafe again and again. Order your favorite idli dipped in finger-licking sambar for some authentic taste.

M-21, M Block Market, Greater Kailash ₹550 for two approx. See Directions

Zambar

The check-in makes you feel like you are in a rice boat from Kerala in the midst of a busy Delhi. Classic taste, authentic menu mentions, and unaltered quality are the cornerstones of Zambar. End your meal with a coconut jaggery pudding and Kerala filter coffee for a complete meal.

Food Court, Level 2, DLF Place Mall, Saket ₹750 for two approx. See Directions
About
According to the 2011 census, Delhi's city proper population was over 11 million, the second-highest in India after Mumbai, while the whole NCT's population was about 16.8 million. Delhi's urban area is now considered to extend beyond the NCT boundaries and include the neighboring satellite cities of Faridabad, Gurgaon, Ghaziabad and Noida in the area, now called Central National Capital Region (CNCR) and has an estimated 2016 population of over 26 million people, making it the world's second-largest urban area according to United Nations.
